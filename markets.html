Our Markets

Discover the 15 unique markets that make up Puces de Saint-Ouen

Explore Our Diverse Markets

Puces de Saint-Ouen is composed of 15 distinct markets, each with its own specialty and atmosphere. From high-end antiques to quirky vintage finds, there's something for every taste and budget.

Marché Vernaison

Allée 1, Rue des Rosiers Sat-Mon: 9am-6pm

The oldest and most picturesque market, established in 1920. Features small, quirky stalls with a bohemian atmosphere. Specializes in small antiques, vintage toys, and collectibles.

Antiques Collectibles Bohemian

Marché Dauphine

132-140 Rue des Rosiers Sat-Mon: 10am-6pm

Two floors of high-quality antiques and vintage items. Known for its professional dealers and carefully curated selection of furniture, art, and decorative objects.

Antiques Furniture Art

Marché Biron

85 Rue des Rosiers Sat-Mon: 10am-6pm

The luxury destination for high-end antiques and fine art. Specializes in 18th and 19th century furniture, paintings, and exceptional decorative pieces.

Luxury Fine Art Furniture

Marché Paul Bert

110 Rue des Rosiers Sat-Mon: 9am-6pm

A favorite of interior designers, offering a mix of antiques and vintage items from the 20th century. Great for mid-century modern furniture and industrial pieces.

Vintage Mid-Century Industrial

Marché Serpette

110 Rue des Rosiers Sat-Mon: 10am-6pm

Specializes in rare and unusual items from the Art Deco and Art Nouveau periods. A treasure trove for collectors of unique design pieces.

Art Deco Art Nouveau Design

Marché Jules Vallès

Avenue Michelet Sat-Mon: 9am-6pm

The place for vintage clothing, textiles, and fashion accessories from the 1920s to the 1980s. A favorite spot for fashion designers and stylists.

Vintage Fashion Textiles Accessories
